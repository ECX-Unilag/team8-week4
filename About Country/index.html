THE THREE REGIONS

NORTHERN NIGERIA

Northern Nigeria is a graphical region of Nigeria, taking up nearly two thirds the land mass of the country. The ethnic groups there are largely Muslim and many are Hausa. This region includes Kogi, Kwara, Benue and Taraba State towards the south. Northern Nigeria was established in 1967.

WESTERN NIGERIA

Western Nigeria is a subdivision of Nigeria, making up the division with Lagos. Western Nigeria was established in 1967 along with the Northern and Southern Regions. The physical setting is,full of rivers and the plant life is limited, except for bunches of cacti once in a while.

EASTERN NIGERIA

Eastern Nigeria is the East most division of Nigeria. Unlike the others, Eastern Nigeria became a section in 1954. The Eastern Region is one of Nigeria's federal divisions, dating back originally from the division of the colony Southern Nigeria in 1954. Its capital is Enugu.
